FY2025 Budget Reduction Strategy — 15% Cut ($2.25M)

Constraint: Cut $2.25M from a $15M budget without weakening security or the cloud-first growth mandate. I explicitly reject blanket "attrition with no backfill" as a primary lever — cutting headcount indiscriminately from a $9M personnel base risks security operations and critical engineering capacity, which violates the mandate itself.

1. Renegotiate Enterprise Licensing (Target: ~$500K)

Run a volume-licensing/EA true-up with Microsoft and Oracle now, before the FY2025 renewal cycle. Audit for shelfware (licenses paid for but unused — common at 500-employee scale) and consolidate overlapping SaaS tools. This is a zero-operational-risk saving: it doesn't touch headcount, security posture, or delivery capacity. It also sets up leverage for the vendor decision in Task 4 (an Azure lean strengthens EA negotiating position).

2. Cloud & Infrastructure Optimization (Target: ~$1.0–1.2M)

Rather than cutting the Hardware/Infrastructure line ($3M) blindly, redirect it: accelerate the cloud-first migration itself as the savings mechanism. Rightsizing, reserved instances/savings plans, and retiring on-prem hardware refresh cycles typically yield 25–35% savings on infrastructure spend. This is the single highest-leverage move because it cuts cost and executes the strategic mandate simultaneously — every dollar saved here is also a dollar of progress against the 18-month cloud-first goal, not a tradeoff against it.

3. Rebalance (Not Slash) Consulting Spend (Target: ~$500–750K)

Don't cut Digital Transformation consulting broadly — that's the growth-critical line. Instead: shift from open-ended staff-augmentation MSP contracts to fixed-price/outcome-based SOWs, and use freed internal capacity (from Phase 1 containerization work on the modernization roadmap) to insource work previously outsourced. Any headcount reduction should be targeted and specific — legacy Solaris/on-prem admin roles that the modernization roadmap is already phasing out by design — not an across-the-board freeze.

Why This Combination Is Sustainable

It avoids the two riskiest levers (blind attrition, cutting security) entirely, and Lever #2 turns the budget cut into a forcing function for the cloud-first strategy rather than a competing priority — the CFO's ask and the Board's mandate reinforce each other instead of conflicting.
